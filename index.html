370 BC - Socrates

Socrates tells a story about two Egyptian gods, Theuth and Thamus, debating the merits of introducing writing to the people. Theuth argues that writing will make people wiser and improve their memory, while Thamus believes it will make people forgetful and overly reliant on the written words. They would become overwhelmed with external information but have no true understanding.

1436 - Gutenberg

Gutenberg's printing press made books much more abundant and affordable, allowing literacy to spread to more people. This wider access to information empowered self-education for many and accelerated the spread of new scientific and cultural ideas, profoundly impacting society. At the same time, the printing press disrupted existing power structures by decentralizing the control of information, which some saw as threatening established institutions and ways of thinking.

2023 - Generative AI

Today, generative AI systems that produce synthetic yet coherent text, images, and more could usher in a new wave of abundant and accessible artificial content and 'creativity.' This may further empower access to information and open up new creative capabilities for many.
